Respiratory System

The respiratory body system primary function is gas exchange to taking the oxygen and releasing carbon dioxide.

In the respiratory body system main functions there are:

  • Gas exchange
  • Breathing
  • Air filtration
  • Protection
  • Sound production

The respiratory body system major organs:

  • Nose → filters, warms, moistens air
  • Throat → passage way for air and food
  • Voice box → contains vocal cords
  • Wind pipe → directs air to the lungs
  • Lungs → gas exchange

Inhalation brings oxygen into the lungs.

Exhalation expels carbon dioxide from the body.

Circulatory System

The circulatory system means to circulates the blood throughout the body and transports oxygen & various vital material each and every cell of the body is called circulatory system.

In the circulatory system main components there are three:

  • Heart
  • Blood vessels
  • Blood

Blood vessels: Blood vessels are tubes that carry blood throughout body. There are three types:

  • Artery (away from the heart)
  • Veins (to the heart)
  • Capillaries (tiny connectors)

Heart: The heart pumps the blood throughout the body. In heart there are four chambers:

  • Left and right atria (upper chamber)
  • Left and right ventricles (lower chamber)

Blood: The blood contains red blood cells (carry oxygen), white blood cells (fight infection), platelets (help with blood clotting), and plasma (the fluid).

Skeletal Body System

The skeletal body system is a structure of the body and it's provided by the protection in internal organs.

The skeletal body system main functions are:

  • Gives shape to our body
  • Protects organs
  • Stores minerals like calcium
  • It supports the body moves

There are 11 Body Systems

  1. Cardiovascular System (Circulatory System): It transports blood, oxygen, nutrients, and waste throughout the body.
  2. Digestive System: This system breaks down food into nutrients and removes waste.
  3. Endocrine System: This system uses hormones to control growth, metabolism, and reproduction.
  4. Integumentary System: This includes skin, hair, and nails. It protects the body.
  5. Lymphatic System: This system helps in immunity, fluid balance, and fat absorption.
  6. Muscular System: This system helps in movement and produces heat.
  7. Nervous System: This is the body’s control center.
  8. Reproductive System: This system is responsible for producing offspring.
  9. Respiratory System: This system helps in breathing (oxygen in, carbon dioxide out).
  10. Skeletal System: This includes bones and cartilage. It gives structure and protection.
  11. Urinary System: This system removes waste from the blood as urine.
