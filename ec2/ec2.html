Spot Instances

Spot Instances use unused AWS EC2 capacity that is available at that time.

They are very low cost compared to other EC2 purchasing options.

AWS can stop or terminate Spot Instances at any time if capacity is needed.

They are mainly used for fault-tolerant and flexible workloads.

Spot Instances are good for batch jobs, data processing, and testing.

They are not suitable for critical or production applications.

On-Demand Instances

On-Demand Instances do not require any long-term commitment.

You pay only for the compute time you actually use.

There is no upfront payment required to start an instance.

This option provides high flexibility to start or stop anytime.

On-Demand is commonly used for testing and short-term workloads.

It is the most expensive EC2 pricing option.

Savings Plans

Savings Plans require a commitment for one or three years.

They apply to EC2, Lambda, and Fargate services.

Savings Plans are more flexible compared to Reserved Instances.

You commit to a fixed hourly usage instead of instance type.

This helps reduce overall AWS compute cost.

They are best for steady and predictable workloads.

Reserved Instances

Reserved Instances require a long-term commitment of one or three years.

They provide significant cost savings compared to On-Demand pricing.

You must choose a specific instance type and region.

Reserved Instances are commonly used for production workloads.

They are suitable for applications running 24/7.

This option offers less flexibility than Savings Plans.

Dedicated Hosts

Dedicated Hosts provide a full physical server for one AWS account.

No other AWS customers share the hardware.

They support Bring Your Own License (BYOL).

You get full control over CPU, memory, and placement.

Dedicated Hosts are used for compliance and licensing needs.

This is the most expensive EC2 option.

Dedicated Instances

Dedicated Instances run on hardware dedicated to one AWS account.

They are not shared with other AWS customers.

You do not get full hardware control like Dedicated Hosts.

They are mainly used when isolation is required.

Dedicated Instances cost more than On-Demand Instances.

They are cheaper than Dedicated Hosts.

Capacity Reservations

Capacity Reservations guarantee EC2 capacity in a specific Availability Zone.

They ensure instances are always available when needed.

No long-term commitment is required.

This option is used for critical and business applications.

You are charged at standard On-Demand pricing.

Capacity Reservations help avoid capacity shortages.
